Pokémon GO Regional Championships

The Pokémon GO Regional Championships are a highly anticipated series of competitive events held worldwide, offering players the chance to showcase their skills in Pokémon GO battles. These championships are the second-largest events in terms of attendance and Championship Point payouts, following the Pokémon International Championships. Participants compete in various categories, including Pokémon TCG, video games, and Pokémon GO, with the opportunity to earn cash prizes, scholarships, and gift cards. The events are open to all players with a Pokémon Trainer Club account in good standing, and registration is typically handled online, often requiring early sign-ups due to high demand. Each championship features main events on Saturdays and Sundays, with side events and smaller competitions on Fridays. The 2025 schedule includes venues across North America, Europe, and beyond, with exciting locations such as the Baltimore Convention Center, Westfalenhallen in Dortmund, and the Henry B. González Convention Center in San Antonio. These championships not only provide a platform for competitive play but also foster a sense of community and camaraderie among Pokémon GO enthusiasts from around the globe.

The Battle formula for the Pokémon GO Regional Championships

In the Pokémon GO Regional Championships, battles are structured within a competitive format where players participate in head-to-head matchups using their Pokémon teams. Trainers must assemble a team of three Pokémon that fall within the predetermined CP limit, often set by the rules of the championship's specific league (Great, Ultra, or Master). Battles are conducted in a best-of-three format to determine the winner of each round. Opponents are chosen through a randomized pairing system initially, ensuring a fair and unbiased competition. As the tournament progresses, a Swiss-style pairing system is often used, where players with similar win-loss records are matched against each other. This method ensures that the most skilled and consistent trainers advance through the rounds. The top-performing players from these preliminary rounds then move on to elimination rounds, culminating in a final showdown to crown the champion. Each battle tests strategy, adaptability, and in-depth knowledge of Pokémon mechanics.
